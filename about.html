About

Donald Trump

Donald Trump is a man who needs no introduction. Being a household name from various experiences, Trump is ready to handle the next stage - not only for himself, but for our country. A full link to Donald Trump's profile sheet can be found here. Donald Trump was born to Mary and Fred Trump on June 14, 1946 in Queens, New York. His father was a successful real-estate mogul in New York which led to Trump’s interest in the business. Trump attended school at the New York Military academy and went off to Fordham University before transferring to the University of Pennsylvania, receiving his degree from the renowned Wharton School of Business. He then later became a successful businessman, much like his father in many different facets. Donald Trump found his success through his own business ventures to include the famous Trump Organization. Trump has been involved with the Republican Party since the Golden Age of Ronald Reagan. He was a staunch supporter of Reagan and met with him several times. Trump has dabbled in the idea of running for president under the reform party. and has polled as high as seven percent against George W. Bush and Al Gore. Despite dropping out, support for him was very high and he was able to win several of his party's primaries.

Stances and supporters

The Issues

Donald Trump's full list of stances on the issues can be found here. A summary is below:

  • Donald Trump is a strong supporter of the Second Amendment, the right of the people to bear Arms shall not be infringed upon. The Second Amendment is a fundamental right which belongs to all law abiding citizens. President Trump will uphold the second amendment at all cost, but if Clinton were elected she would try to limit the rights of the people of America. Donald Trump is the defender of rights of the gun owning Americans of this great country.
  • President Trump has a 7 point plan to improve trade relations in America’s favor and bring back jobs to the U.S. President Trump would appoint tough trade negotiators to fight for better trade relations with countries such as China, Mexico and South Korea. Trump will confront China on its violations of trade deals with America and its illegal activities against the
    American people and force China to change its trade agreements if it continues these activities.
  • President Trump sees the best way to make America Great Again is to invest in American students through school choice. President Trump also plans to work with congress to ensure that universities lower cost of college and student debt in order to make it easier for students to access, pay for, and finish a college education.
  • President Trump plans on prioritizing the jobs, wages, and security of American citizens. President Trump plans on building a wall to prevent illegal immigration to protect American wages. President Trump also plans on reforming legal immigration to promote American workers by keeping immigration levels at the normal level.
